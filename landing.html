Built for people who take
a lot of screenshots.

Every feature you wish your file explorer had — search by text, blur sensitive data, annotate before sharing.

Full-text OCR Search

Uses Windows Native OCR to extract text from every screenshot. Search by anything — an error message, a URL, a price — and find it instantly.

Auto-Watch & Index

Point Kaptur at your Screenshots folder and forget about it. Every new file is instantly detected, hashed for deduplication, and indexed automatically.

Smart Blur Regions

Automatically detects passwords, OTP codes, and emails. Draw, move, and resize blur zones manually. Double-click to remove in edit mode.

Annotation Tools

Arrow, Box, and Highlight tools with a full color picker. Mark up screenshots before sharing — annotations are baked into the exported file.

Drag-and-Drop Sharing

Drag a screenshot from Kaptur directly into Slack, Teams, email, or any upload field. The blurred + annotated version is always what gets sent.

Protected Copy & Export

Copy blurred images to clipboard or save as a permanent PNG with all protections applied. Export your entire vault as a ZIP from Settings.

Wishlist / Favorites

Heart any screenshot to add it to your Wishlist. One-click filter shows only your favorited shots in the main grid.

Instant Overlay

Press Ctrl+Shift+F anywhere to summon a floating quick-search overlay without leaving your workflow.

Undo Support

Step-by-step undo (Ctrl+Z) for every annotation drawn in edit mode. Made a mistake with the highlighter? Just undo it.

Simple setup

Up and running in 3 steps.

No account. No config files. No cloud setup.

01

Download & Install

Download the .msi installer, run it, and Kaptur appears in your system tray. No Rust or Node.js required for end users.

02

Point it at your folder

Open Settings and choose your Screenshots folder. Kaptur begins watching and indexing immediately — all existing files are picked up on the first scan.

03

Search, blur, share

Take a screenshot as usual. Kaptur detects it instantly, runs OCR, and it appears in your grid ready to search, blur sensitive content, annotate, and drag-share without ever uploading a pixel to the cloud.
